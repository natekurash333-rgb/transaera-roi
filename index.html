The thermodynamic thesis

Why the savings number holds

A conventional DX DOAS overcools to wring out latent moisture, then burns electric reheat to drag supply air back to neutral. The MOF wheel pre-dries the air — cutting compressor work and all but eliminating the reheat penalty. Model both streams or you understate the case.

Baseline context · conservative Northern profile

These numbers run a deliberately conservative 15% reheat firing fraction and Northern utility tariffs — the climate I model with absolute authority. In a target ERCOT / Gulf-Coast market, baseline DX DOAS reheat doesn't fire 15% of the time; it runs near-continuously wringing moisture out of 80°F-dewpoint air. The reheat-avoidance bucket — already the larger of the two streams — expands geometrically, and the payback compresses well below the 1.95-year figure shown. The model is sandbagged against my home turf; your market makes it stronger.

03

The portfolio instrument

10-year EaaS · where the structure earns its keep

The payback ratio is the same ~1.95 years at any size — bigger tonnage doesn't lengthen it. The long-dated EaaS is a portfolio instrument because of a hard supply-side floor: financiers won't underwrite below a ~$50–100k minimum deal size. Clear that floor by aggregating buildings, and two things unlock — the deal is worth papering, and the developer gets the whole premium off-balance-sheet as OpEx.

The beachhead

Congregate residential is where the split-incentive dies and the EaaS wins.

University housing, senior living, K-12, and adaptive-reuse multifamily share the structure third-party ownership was built for: a developer who won't spend CapEx on a premium they don't operate, and a ventilation load the MOF wheel is thermodynamically built to crush. The unit economics are the proof; the portfolio is the product.

Upside not modeled in the numbers above

§179D is a deduction (≈ value × marginal rate), up to ~$5/sf at prevailing wage — and often allocable to the designer when an adaptive-reuse owner's liability is already consumed by Historic and LIHTC credits. Layer NYS Clean Heat and similar state decarbonization grants on top. None of it is in the payback — because you don't hand a developer a tax number their counsel hasn't blessed. It widens the margin; it doesn't carry the deal.
